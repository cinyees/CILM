Personal sentiment
In most group projects, it is inevitable that the urge to override your teammates' suggestions arises because they do not align with your vision. However, it is imperative to find a middle ground - each member thinks that their ideas are just as viable as you think yours are, and therefore see value in their own contributions. Throughout the module, and prior to the group presentation, I realised through the cohort's revelations that your academic and cultural background affected the way that you thought about certain topics, each culture having their own idea about what was conservative, what was controversial and morality. This was important to the final presentations - with each of the group mates coming from different places, individual contributions to the work were particularly important to understand how to address the topic of environmental disasters in a way that was both informative, yet culturally sensitive and empathetic.
