An essay! Finally some familiarity!

Finally, a method of assessment you are familiar with! Your experience in this method of assessment brings you back to your comfort zone. You're exhausted from being lost throughout the more abstract exercises, and did not see the point. However...

The final assessment is a reflective piece you are encouraged to be creative with.
You:




Upon contemplation, you realise....
Writing an essay is familiar to you, and straightforward. You can duly express your thoughts without worrying that your message won't be received. Any other mediums will have to be explained anyway, and the marker may not receive your approach the way you want them to. The risk of using a different medium to express how you felt throughout the module will be risky...

However, you have a seedling of an idea. You have recently picked up the skill of basic coding through an extra-curricular, learning how to code your own website and add components to it. How about presenting the journey through the module as a series of choices to be encountered? That way, you can almost make the marker take the module, presenting them with the same choices and thoughts that you were faced with. What better way to put the marker in your shoes? However, it will still be risky: you have no one to ask for help if your code fails you....
