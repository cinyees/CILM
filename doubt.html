Personal Sentiment
Whilst in group projects there is always a temptation to gain a free ride, especially if you observe that one of your group mates is already doing this, and that there is no peer assessment for this project. However, to get an unsatisfactory grade knowing that you could have brought it up is a dubious work ethic. From past group projects, I learned that it is better to put in your best effort despite the behaviour of other group mates - at the end of the day, it is your own grade that you are sacrificing just for temporary ease. Additionally, I realise that such situations are bound to arise in many work situations - it is inevitable that there will be group work with people you are unfamiliar with, and that similar learning experiences at university will help me to understand the most efficient way to work and compromise with your groupmates.

Whilst one of our group members was absent, and another seriously ill, the rest of the group performed to the best of our abilities. Though some of our group members were unsatisfied with the mark, I thought it was reflective of what we could achieve with the limiting circumstances. I believed that there was no unfairness in this, but that these were circumstances that arise in real life, and we did what we could do.
