You read the paper.

The presentations at the critical discussion exercise come easy to you, and you are able to raise counterpoints as you present the author's points. Your seminar tutors are impressed, and tell you how you could improve, and give you extra dimensions to critique. When it comes to the critical essay, you have a good idea of what to include, and how to be concise. Putting your knowledge to good use, you achieve a high grade for your essay.

After the seminar, your tutor allocates you into groups for your assessed presentations. You are not close to any of your members. You:




Having had a think, you realise...
Although these are your peers, you are only close to a handful of them, and don't feel comfortable sharing a personal part of your life with those you are only aquainted with. You know that you could make up something superficial about your picture, and quickly get the exercise over with - what are the real consequences anyway? Maintaining your guard is easy. Opening up is not.

However, this may be a good opportunity to break the ice with some of your classmates, and let the seminar tutor know a little bit about who you are through your picture, and the way you express what you want to share. Maybe this is an opportunity to grow - this may be useful in the workplace in the future among relationships that you may have to forge. Why not pick up the skill of becoming closer to people faster?
